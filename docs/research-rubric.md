# Research Briefing Rubric

Use this rubric for all daily and weekly market-intelligence outputs in the AI deployment tracker project.

## 1) Signal strength
Score each item by materiality:
- **5 — Major move**: launch, GA, big pricing change, major partnership, funding, or a clearly new product category.
- **4 — Strong move**: meaningful feature release, major docs/changelog update, notable enterprise/customer traction, or visible ecosystem expansion.
- **3 — Moderate move**: credible incremental update that matters to active users or buyers.
- **2 — Weak signal**: small release, minor wording change, or ambiguous marketing update.
- **1 — Noise**: repeated announcement, low-confidence rumor, or generic content.

## 2) Dedupe rules
Treat an item as a duplicate if:
- it is the same company + same product/feature/topic, and
- the new evidence does not add a new material fact.

When deduping:
- keep only the newest or most specific version,
- merge supporting sources,
- mark the item as **ongoing** instead of repeating it verbatim,
- only re-surface it if there is a new material fact.

## 3) Relevance rules
Prioritize items that show:
- new product launches
- new features or major capability jumps
- pricing or packaging changes
- partnerships and integrations
- enterprise/customer traction
- funding or hiring that signals momentum
- docs/changelog activity that points to real roadmap movement

## 4) Adjacent-sector selection
A sector is worth tracking if it has at least one of:
- repeatable public signals
- frequent product launches
- clear developer or buyer demand
- visible funding or ecosystem momentum
- obvious overlap with deployment / infra / developer tools

## 5) Output style
For daily briefs:
- keep the output short
- highlight only material changes
- avoid repeating unchanged items

For weekly briefs:
- merge the two daily briefings
- dedupe overlap
- emphasize trend changes, not raw lists
- add 1–3 clear takeaways and 1 recommended action
